# 🛠️ Maintenance Logic: Extending the Lifecycle of High-End Knitwear(3 Steps to Make Your Cashmere Last 10 Years)

## 1. System Overview: The Pilling "Feature"
In the realm of textile engineering, pilling is not a bug; it is an inherent physical property of high-grade, long-staple animal fibers. 

* **Mechanism:** Kinetic friction triggers the interlocking of microscopic scales.
* **Authentication:** Zero-pilling usually indicates a "corrupted" material (acid-stripped fibers or synthetic injections).
* **Objective:** This document provides a standardized protocol to manage surface "noise" while preserving the garment's thermal and structural "hardware."

## 2. The "Wear-Rest" Algorithm (Load Balancing)
Continuous wear leads to fiber fatigue. To prevent structural degradation, implement a **Rotation Protocol**:

| Parameter | Value | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wear Cycle** | 1 Day | Active duty period. |
| **Cool-down** | 2 Days | Rest period for fiber elasticity recovery. |
| **Logic** | `Wait(48h)` | Prevents the irreversible stretching of protein chains. |

## 3. Washing Protocol (Data Cleaning)
Improper washing is the leading cause of "System Crash" (shrinkage/felting).

> **Warning:** Never use the `hang_dry` command. Gravity will cause a vertical stretch, leading to a "Silhouette Distortion" bug.

## 4. Surface Refactoring (De-pilling)
Surface pilling is merely a "temporary cache" of loose fibers. It can be cleared without affecting the core performance of the garment.

* **Tool Selection:** Low-power electric shaver or high-density fiber comb.
* **Execution:** Glide horizontally across the surface. Do not apply high pressure to avoid damaging the base knit structure.
* **Frequency:** Periodic maintenance every 3-5 wear cycles.
